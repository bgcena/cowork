Trump calls PM Modi 'friend,' committed to stronger India-US ties: Ambassador Gor

Sergio Gor said the relationship between the two leaders dated back to Trump's first term in office and continued to benefit the broader strategic partnership.

"The United States wants to work hand in hand with India. We care about this relationship. We have a president who deeply cares about this relationship." — US Ambassador Sergio Gor

Washington: US President Donald Trump considers Prime Minister Narendra Modi "a friend" and remains deeply committed to strengthening ties with India, US Ambassador to India Sergio Gor said, describing the personal rapport between the two leaders as an important asset in advancing the bilateral relationship.

Speaking at the US-India Strategic Partnership Forum (USISPF) Leadership Summit, Gor said President Trump continued to hold India in high regard and frequently spoke about his experiences in the country.

"I was just with him before the weekend started here in DC and I could spend about two hours with the president and he was captivated by what I have seen in India. He has very fond memories of India. His visit last time, it was one of his most remarkable visits that he continues to talk about. He holds India very fondly." — US Ambassador to India, Sergio Gor

UFC Call — A Story of Friendship

Highlighting the close personal chemistry between the two leaders, Gor recalled an incident from a recent Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) event in Miami.

"I was a couple months ago... with the president at UFC in Miami and we're sitting backstage and he said to me, 'Let's call the Prime Minister.'" — Ambassador Gor

Although the call was eventually scheduled for the following day, Gor said the episode illustrated the true nature of the relationship between Trump and PM Modi.

"The message there, the louder message of that story is when you're friends with somebody, not everything has to be scheduled." — Ambassador Gor

Deeper Ties Rooted in History

Gor said the relationship between the two leaders dated back to Trump's first term in office and continued to benefit the broader strategic partnership between the two nations.

"His years with the Prime Minister go back to his first term. There's warm memories of India. And so that's an incredible benefit." — Ambassador Gor

Gor also expressed hope that President Trump would visit India again, saying he looked forward to facilitating such a visit as part of deepening the bilateral engagement.

"I look forward to at some point... having the president visit us back in India." — Ambassador Gor
